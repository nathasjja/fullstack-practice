Smoor is a comforting Indonesian beef stew with Dutch colonial influences. It’s a slow-cooked dish where beef is simmered with sweet soy sauce, nutmeg, and spices until tender and flavorful.

Ingredients

  • 500g beef (chuck or brisket), cut into cubes
  • 2 onions, thinly sliced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp kecap manis (sweet soy sauce)
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp ground nutmeg
  • 1 tsp ground cloves
  • 2 tbsp butter or oil
  • Salt & pepper to taste
  • Water or beef stock (enough to cover meat)

Instructions

  1. Heat butter/oil in a pot. Sauté onions and garlic until fragrant.
  2. Add beef cubes, browning them on all sides.
  3. Stir in tomatoes, nutmeg, cloves, bay leaves, kecap manis, and soy sauce.
  4. Pour in enough water or stock to cover the beef.
  5. Simmer gently on low heat for 1.5–2 hours until beef is tender.
  6. Adjust seasoning with salt, pepper, and more kecap manis if needed.
  7. Serve hot with steamed rice or bread.
